Doom Prevails

Reducing dead draws through synergy

Two engines, one clean audit. A card is a dead draw when it feeds neither Doctor Doom's land-discard drain nor Loki's copy loop. The cleanest of the four to audit.

The deck wants two things: throw away lands to drain the table, and copy villains with Loki. Any card that helps with neither gets swapped out.
Superior Foes of Spider-Man → Green Goblin, Revenant
Molecule Man → Kingpin, Wilson Fisk
Damocles Base, Sword of Kang → Sundial of the Infinite

The Fantastic Four

Mana fixing and color consistency

One tax, and it is color. The only four-color deck wants its fixing early, so two cheap untapped Talismans carry the color load.

This deck needs four colors of mana at once. So we add cards that make color and mana, which is the deck's one hard part.
Willie Lumpkin, Postman → Talisman of Creativity
Mirage Mirror → Talisman of Conviction
Into the Time Vortex → West Coast Expansion

Avengers Assemble

The case for simpler decks

The friendliest deck wins by being easy to read. Keep the go-wide counters plan legible while you make it more consistent.

This is the easy deck to understand: heroes get counters and swing. The upgrades keep it easy to follow while making it stronger.
Love on the Battlefield → Champions from Beyond
Avenge → Damning Verdict
Gift of Immortality → Wave Goodbye

Wakanda Forever

When a good card is the wrong choice

Raw power is one test among several. Cut the strong card whose real cost is the target it paints, and serve the Vibranium and counters plan.

A card can be strong and still be wrong for the deck. We cut the flashy cards that fight the plan and keep the ones that fit it.
Coveted Jewel → Batterskull
Gilded Lotus → Reprieve
Sword of the Animist → Verdurous Gearhulk
