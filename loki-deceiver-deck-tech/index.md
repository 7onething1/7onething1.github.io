# Loki, the Deceiver: The Copy Engine, Built Out

The second commander in the Doom Prevails box copies one of your Villains on every attack. This is the whole deck: which Villains are worth copying, how the copy behaves under the rules, and a full upgrade path off the precon.

## What the copy can and cannot do

When Loki attacks, you create a tapped and attacking copy of another target Villain you control. It is an Illusion, it is not legendary, and it is sacrificed at the next end step. Whenever one or more of your Villains deal combat damage to a player, you draw a card.

The copy enters already attacking, and that decides which abilities pay off:

- **Enter abilities** fire, because the token enters.
- **Static abilities** apply, because the token is on the battlefield.
- **Combat-damage abilities** fire, because the token can connect.
- **Ordinary attack triggers** do not fire. Comprehensive Rule 508.3a: a "whenever this creature attacks" ability triggers only when the creature is declared as an attacker, and it "won't trigger if a creature is put onto the battlefield attacking." Wizards gives the same official ruling for Kaalia of the Vast.

One narrow exception: an ability reading "whenever this creature attacks and isn't blocked" follows Rule 509.3g and can still trigger during the declare-blockers step for a creature that entered attacking, as long as no blockers are declared for it. A plain "whenever this creature attacks" gets nothing on the copy.

Loki's draw counts once per damaged player. Five Villains hitting one opponent draw a single card, and one evasive Villain against each of three opponents draws three. Spread your attackers when the board allows it.

## How Loki closes a game

Value is the engine, and the finish is combat. The other two plays feed that attack rather than replace it. Pick the plan at the start of every game, because a deck that only draws cards can durdle forever without closing a table.

- **The finish: combat through temporary multiplication.** Copy Mysterio for a board of 3/3 Villains, or Doctor Octopus to swing the whole team bigger, and point evasive Villains at different seats so Loki draws two or three off the one combat. The copied Mysterio's tokens vanish at your end step, so the burst has to be aimed for lethal in the turn you make it. Most games end here, one seat at a time.
- **Kingpin Treasure feeds the finish.** Copy Kingpin, let both Kingpins see the end-step sacrifice for four Treasure, and that mana pays for a Doctor Doom drain, a second haymaker, or the wrath-and-rebuild. It manufactures the resources for the closing attack rather than ending the game on its own.
- **The reset buys another attack.** When behind, Blasphemous Act or Toxic Deluge clears the board, and the next Loki swing rebuilds a copied threat while the connive pieces refill your hand. The reset buys a fresh run at the combat finish, and in a long grind the deck that keeps drawing gets there first.

The honest read: this is a fair midrange attrition deck with no single-card kill. That is a strength in a casual pod and a weakness against a fast go-wide start. Against a slow table, lean on combat and card draw. Against a fast one, hold a wrath and win the rebuild.

## Five copy targets that pay off, and one trap

- **Enter abilities.** Tombstone, Career Criminal returns a Villain from your graveyard, Tri-Sentinel, Act of Vengeance deals 3 to a creature for each opponent as a 7/7 flyer, and Mysterio, Master of Illusion makes a 3/3 Illusion Villain token for each nontoken Villain you control (those tokens are exiled when the copy leaves at your end step, so they are enter-trigger fuel for that turn unless Sundial keeps the copy).
- **Battlefield payoffs.** Doctor Octopus, Master Planner gives your other Villains +2/+2 and refills your hand to eight, a static effect that applies while the token is present. Iron Monger, Sadistic Tycoon is triggered, not static: whenever a creature connives it puts a counter on each Villain, so a copied Iron Monger means both fire on every connive that turn.
- **Combat-damage abilities.** Klaw, Master of Sound has deathtouch and pays off on a hit, and Stilt-Man, Towering Terror takes control of a noncreature, nonland permanent until the end of your next turn (a temporary theft, and it cannot be sacrificed while borrowed) whenever your Villains deal that player combat damage, its damage clause sharing Loki's exact wording.
- **Evasion.** Prowler, Clawed Thief has menace and connives on a Villain enter, Red Ghost, Intangible Genius cannot be blocked and carries ward, and Moonstone, Harsh Mistress flies.
- **Clones.** Chameleon, Master of Disguise enters as a copy of a creature you control, so you pick your best Villain as it enters (the choice is on entry, so recursion gets the pick too), and Loki copying Chameleon repeats whatever form it currently holds (a second of that creature). Left in its base shape, Chameleon hands Loki's token the choice instead. Spark Double works the same way, so a Spark Double that is currently a Doctor Octopus gives Loki a second Doctor Octopus rather than a new pick. Titan of Littjara is not a clone or a changeling: it chooses a creature type as it enters (name Villain), so it belongs with the enter abilities, drawing a card for each of your other Villains.
- **The attack-trigger trap.** Ultron, Unlimited, Killmonger, Ruthless Usurper, and Helmut Zemo, Mastermind all read "whenever this creature attacks," so a copy gets nothing from them. Keep them as bodies you cast and attack the honest way, and copy the five categories above.

## Copy targets, ranked

When more than one copy is legal, reach for the one nearest the top. Sorted by how much a single rented turn buys.

| Rank | What you are buying | Best targets | Why it is worth the copy |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Temporary board multiplication | Mysterio, Master of Illusion | A 3/3 Villain for each Villain you control, entering at once for one oversized attack. The tokens clear when the copied Mysterio leaves at end step, a one-turn burst. Feeds Prowler, Iron Monger, and every sac outlet that turn. |
| 2 | Removal on enter | Tri-Sentinel, Act of Vengeance | 3 damage to a creature for each opponent as it enters, then swings as a 7/7 flying menace trampler. |
| 3 | Extra cards | Doctor Octopus; Tombstone | Doc Ock refills your hand to eight and buffs the team. Tombstone rebuys a Villain from the yard each attack. |
| 4 | Burst mana | Kingpin, Wilson Fisk | Four Treasure off the end-step sacrifice when both Kingpins fire, the fuel for your closing turn. |
| 5 | Reliable draw pressure | Prowler; Red Ghost; Moonstone | Evasion usually connects through an open lane, turning Loki's damage trigger into a card, though a blocker, removal, or a fog can still stop it. |
| 6 | Political theft | Stilt-Man; Klaw | Stilt-Man steals a noncreature permanent from anyone your Villains hit. Klaw carries deathtouch. Narrowest, still real. |

Two lanes Grixis barely fills, pure protection and hard removal on a body, come from spells in the 99 instead of copies. When the top three targets are on the board, copy for temporary multiplication or removal before you copy for one card.

**Baron Strucker, HYDRA Overlord** sits outside this table on purpose. His cost reduction is static and his connive fires only when other Villains enter, so a one-turn combat copy does almost nothing. Cast him as the engine that cheapens your Villains, and spend Loki's copy on a target that pays off in combat.

## Loki's Scepter, the borrowed face

Loki's Scepter (about 74 percent) taps for any color and, on enter, gains control of a creature until end of turn, untaps it, gives it haste, and makes it a Villain. Steal an opponent's best creature, turn it into a Villain, and copy it on the same attack. The Scepter's Villain type is a real type change on the original, so the borrowed creature is a legal copy target and, as a Villain this turn, draws you a card whenever it connects. The token is where the paths split: a copy uses copiable values only, the printed characteristics plus copy effects, and a temporary type from the Scepter is a non-copy effect, so Comprehensive Rule 707.2 keeps that Villain type off the token. Loki's token carries the creature's printed types plus Illusion, a Villain only when the stolen creature is a printed Villain. Steal an ordinary creature for one dependable draw off the original and a bonus body that does not draw; steal a printed Villain and both draw against separate opponents.

## Keeping Loki alive to swing

The precon ships Swiftfoot Boots; the first upgrade is Lightning Greaves. Haste matters because Loki cannot make his copy the turn he enters without it, though his card draw still works whenever your other Villains connect. Two equipment pieces is the honest boundary of this adaptation: the Boots and Greaves get Loki his first attack, and the deck stays open to instant-speed removal and equipment disruption. A wide Villain board does not shield Loki by itself, and the jobs differ: a removal spell clears a blocker, but answering a spell aimed at Loki needs a counter or an instant-speed dodge. This revision raises protection from two pieces to four: it adds Whispersilk Cloak for shroud and an unblockable Loki, and a held Swan Song to answer the removal spell aimed at him. Hold the counter for the crack-back. One sequencing note the shroud creates: Lightning Greaves and Whispersilk Cloak give Loki shroud, which blocks your own targeting as well, so finish any equip or targeted effect on Loki before you attach either one, and slide the Greaves off with its free equip whenever you need to target him again. Swiftfoot Boots grants hexproof instead, so it never blocks your own targeting.

## The upgrade swaps

Who says what: the method is Salubrious Snail's dead-draw audit, and every specific swap, count, and rules-timing note is our own analysis, verified against Scryfall and the Comprehensive Rules, since Snail never covered this precon. The audit: hold every precon card against the Loki copy loop and cut what it cannot use. Every swap stays inside the Grixis color identity and keeps the deck at ninety-nine. A second pass then trades four narrow cards for two more ramp pieces and two more protection pieces.

**Copy targets and payoffs**
- Superior Foes of Spider-Man → Taskmaster, Mercenary Mimic. Superior Foes does nothing as a Loki copy. Taskmaster, in fifty-nine percent of recorded Loki decks, is the sharper Villain and the direct upgrade over the old Green Goblin slot: at your first main phase he becomes a copy of the best creature on any battlefield or in any graveyard until your next turn, so, played a turn ahead, he wears the face of Doctor Octopus, Mysterio, or an opponent's bomb by your next first main phase; casting him during a main phase means that trigger waits until your following turn. Loki copying Taskmaster repeats whatever form he holds, exactly like Chameleon and Spark Double, so he lives with the clones. Green Goblin, an attack-trigger body a Loki token cannot fire, leaves the Loki-focused tier.
- Molecule Man → Kingpin, Wilson Fisk. Molecule Man is a free-cast build-around for a different deck and does nothing for the copy engine at six mana, so it yields to Kingpin, which makes two Treasure the first time you sacrifice a creature each turn. Copy Kingpin himself and both Kingpins see the token's end-step sacrifice, so unless one already fired that turn you get four Treasure, two from each.
- Puppet Master, String Puller → Doctor Octopus, Master Planner. The best static copy target: +2/+2 to every other Villain, hand refills to eight.
- Living Laser → Mysterio, Master of Illusion. An enter-trigger bomb that doubles your board when copied.

**Card advantage and ramp**
- Currency Converter → Phyrexian Arena. The closest call: Currency Converter has real discard synergy with the Doom face, so you give up genuine value, but Phyrexian Arena draws a predictable card every upkeep with no other engine online. Keep the Converter if your build relies on discard.
- Doom's Time Platform → Read the Bones, which scrys two and then draws two for clean selection.
- Progenitor's Icon → Fellwar Stone, a two-mana rock that usually gives strong multicolor fixing, limited to the colors your opponents' lands can make.
- Batroc the Leaper → Mind Stone, early ramp that cashes out for a card once you flood.

**Protection and recursion**
- Patchwork Banner → Lightning Greaves. Haste and protection for the one creature you cannot lose.
- Extract Power → Reanimate. A six-mana untap spell becomes a one-mana graveyard return that reliably finds a strong target. This corrects the earlier list, which wrongly cut Moonstone, Harsh Mistress for Reanimate. Moonstone stays: it is an evasive flying Villain that turns every discard into playable material, so a generic spell leaves for Reanimate instead.

**Second-pass refinements: ramp and protection**
- Age of Ultron → Wayfarer's Bauble. A narrow five-mana saga becomes one-mana fixing that fetches a basic, ramp that helps every hand.
- Kang Dynasty → Charcoal Diamond. A Kang-dependent saga becomes a two-mana black rock, lifting ramp from six to eight.
- Archnemesis → Whispersilk Cloak. A narrow aura becomes protection and evasion in one: Loki cannot be blocked or targeted.
- Propaganda → Swan Song. A pillowfort tax becomes a one-mana counter for the removal spell aimed at Loki, the fourth protection slot.
- Damocles Base, Sword of Kang → Sundial of the Infinite. Keeps a copy forever: let the end step begin so the sacrifice trigger goes on the stack, then activate Sundial in response to end the turn. Fire it early and the sacrifice only waits for the next end step.

## Buying upgrades: theme or power

The swaps do two different jobs. Some adds deepen the Loki fantasy, a villain army you copy for one explosive turn. Others are cards any Grixis deck wants, generic draw, fixing, and protection that raise the floor. Buy from the column that matches why you picked this deck.

| Identity adds (keep the copy fantasy) | Power adds (raise any Grixis deck) |
|---|---|
| Doctor Octopus, the best static copy target | Phyrexian Arena, a card every upkeep |
| Mysterio, the board-doubling copy | Read the Bones, scry two then draw two |
| Kingpin, the four-Treasure copy loop | Fellwar Stone and Mind Stone, cheap fixing and ramp |
| Sundial of the Infinite, keeps a copy forever | Lightning Greaves and Whispersilk Cloak, cheap protection |
| Taskmaster, a clone of your best Villain from your next main phase | Reanimate, a one-mana reach for any big body |

If you bought Doom Prevails for the swingy Loki turn, the left column is your shopping list. If you bought it to have a tuned Grixis deck, start with the right column, since those cards win games no matter which face is out. A player upgrading in order buys Doctor Octopus and Mysterio first, then the mana and card advantage.

## The Doom side of the box

The precon's front face is Doctor Doom, King of Latveria, a discard-and-drain commander; Loki is the alternate. Keep Doom or run the deck as printed and the discard cards stay strong. Build Loki as sole commander and the deck tilts toward copy value, so the pure discard-support cards leave first. Connive is the bridge: Baron Strucker, Prowler, and Glorious Purpose reward both faces, since every copy Loki makes is another Villain entering.

## Keep or ship: reading an opening hand

Loki never enters the mulligan math, because the commander is not a card in your deck. He begins in the command zone and is always available to cast on turn four, so you never keep or ship a hand for him. The other ninety-nine cards make the library, and a keepable hand wants what actually varies: enough lands for four mana, an early Villain or a rock, and enough card flow that the board is worth copying by the time Loki swings.

| Hand shape | Call | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Two or three lands, an early Villain, some card flow | Keep | The ideal hand: you curve into a board and cast Loki from the command zone on four. |
| Ramp and lands, no Villain yet | Keep if it draws | A rock plus Night's Whisper or Black Market Connections digs to a Villain fast. Ship a pure ramp hand that cannot dig. |
| Several Villains, no acceleration | Keep | The curve peaks at three, so a land drop each turn reaches Loki on four while you build the board. |
| Lands and protection, thin on threats | Keep on the play | Two or three lands plus Swiftfoot Boots or a held counter still casts Loki safely on four; the board comes off the top. |
| One land, or seven lands and no action | Ship | Grixis three-color cannot function on one land, and an all-land hand does nothing. Mulligan for a hand that has two or three lands and a play. |

The short version: keep a hand with two or three lands, at least one two-or-three-drop or a rock, and some card flow. Loki is a given every game, so judge the hand only on whether it builds a board and reaches four mana.

## An opening sequence

1. Land. Cast Sol Ring if you drew it, the only rock playable off one land.
2. A two-mana rock: Arcane Signet, a Talisman, Mind Stone, or Fellwar Stone.
3. A three-mana Villain (Tombstone, Klaw, Prowler, Baron Strucker), or Black Market Connections.
   (Loki arrives turn four; without haste his first attack is turn five, sooner only if a turn-one Sol Ring or an extra rock funds an equip.)
4. Cast Loki for four. When a turn-one Sol Ring or a spare rock also funds an equip this turn, take that line and swing the same turn, since the point of haste is a turn-four attack.
5. Otherwise Loki attacks next turn: equip, swing, copy your best board Villain (Mysterio, Tri-Sentinel, or Doctor Octopus), and draw once for each player your Villains hit.

## What EDHREC can and cannot tell you

EDHREC's synergy percentage measures how often a card appears in submitted Loki decks compared with other decks that could run it. That is deckbuilding popularity. It is not a record of what players copy during combat, because EDHREC tracks cards included in lists rather than in-game target choices. So the figures point you toward popular includes and no further: Tombstone 86 percent, Chameleon 82 percent, Baron Strucker 79 percent, Loki's Scepter 74 percent, Villainous Hideout 71 percent, Prowler 69 percent, Doctor Octopus 68 percent, Taskmaster 59 percent. Loki records about 1,263 decks as of 2026-07-13, a total climbing fast this soon after the June 2026 release, so read it as a dated snapshot rather than a settled figure. Use the list to find strong cards, then choose your copy targets from the five productive categories. Snail's EDHREC Effect video warns the site inflates already-popular cards and overrates pieces that shine only when the commander is out, so keep a few Villains that work on a turn Loki is gone.

## The one rule

Run bodies that pay off the moment they arrive. An enter ability, a battlefield payoff whether static or triggered, a combat-damage hit, or evasion makes the copy worth it. A plain attack trigger gets nothing. Ask what the rented copy does before it is sacrificed; if the answer falls in one of the five working categories, keep it.
