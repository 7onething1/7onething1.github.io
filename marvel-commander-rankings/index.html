How the Overall score is built (the exact weights)

Overall = 0.28 · Out-of-box power + 0.26 · Upgrade ceiling + 0.24 · Fun + 0.12 · Ease + 0.10 · Table politics. Every read is scored 1 to 10, then blended by those weights, so a close placement is a close blend and not an arranged order. Two of the reads need a definition, since they carry real weight: Fun is how much variety, dramatic swing, and live decision-making the deck offers across a game, not its win rate. Table politics is how the pod treats the deck, how quickly it draws targeting, and how much it can threaten, deal, or share, which is why the most powerful list is not always the one you want in a four-player game. Fun carries nearly the weight of power on purpose: these are precons bought for casual, kitchen-table games, where how much a deck is worth playing counts almost as much as how hard it hits.

Rankings fact-check · EDHREC build popularity (captured 13 July 2026)

Deck counts below were captured from EDHREC on 13 July 2026, not read live on load, so treat them as a dated snapshot that measures adoption rather than strength. Every precon ships with more than one legendary commander, so both are listed here, and the Fantastic Four, which ships with four, lists all four:

  • Avengers Assemble Captain America, Team Leader 2,456 · Director Nick Fury 292
  • Wakanda Forever T'Challa, the Black Panther 2,112 · Shuri, the Black Panther 117

Doom is the most-built lead of the four, which tells you he is the most adopted, not that he leads on out-of-box strength. Two places our strict ordering trades against raw popularity: Captain America is built far more than Director Nick Fury (2,456 to 292), yet Fury is the configuration that makes the unchanged Avengers list strongest, so the most-played face is not the strongest stock commander. And the Fantastic Four's leads are the least built of the four decks, which fits its profile as the highest ceiling but the weakest and hardest deck to pilot straight out of the box. We draw the cut-and-add lists from the Draftsim and Gathering Games upgrade guides, so those guides are our source for swaps, not for this four-deck ordering, which is our own read. Is Team Leader best out of the box?

Captain America, Team Leader is the best face commander for the strongest unchanged deck. He is not the strongest commander available inside that box. Director Nick Fury makes the stock Avengers list stronger. Fury costs three mana, supplies continuing Hero selection, and supports the deck's dense Hero count without needing an established battlefield. Captain America supplies haste and vigilance, which create faster pressure. His value falls sharply after removal clears the board.

Team Leader is the easiest Avengers commander to play. Nick Fury actually makes the deck stronger, because he makes your heroes cheaper and keeps finding you more.

Avengers Assemble ranks first among the unchanged Marvel precons. Captain America, Team Leader provides the strongest straightforward experience. Director Nick Fury produces the deck's strongest overall configuration through cheaper deployment and repeatable card advantage.
