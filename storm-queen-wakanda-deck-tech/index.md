# Storm, Queen of Wakanda: The Power Lever

A deck tech for **Storm, Queen of Wakanda**, the Selesnya Wakanda Forever alt, read with Storm in the command zone. **Storm, Queen of Wakanda** costs {3}{G}{W}, a 4/5 Legendary Creature (Mutant Noble Hero), Selesnya. Three abilities that all read off her power: **Flying**; **whenever Storm attacks, another target attacking creature gains flying and gets +X/+0, where X is Storm's power**; and **whenever a creature with flying attacks you, Storm deals damage equal to her power to it**. She is a Wakanda Forever alt, a green-white flyers deck that pours every buff into one commander and lets that single number run the offense, the evasion handoff, and the air defense at once.

## The engine, one number three ways

Storm turns her power into three effects. On offense, the attack trigger adds her power to a second creature and grants flying, so one pump becomes two air threats. On defense, she deals her power to any flyer that attacks you, a repeatable answer to decks that come from above. And her own body flies, so her power is a commander-damage clock. Every point added to Storm improves all three. Most creature decks spread buffs across a wide board; Storm asks the opposite, concentrate the buffs on the commander because her power is the multiplier on all three abilities.

## The power lever

X is Storm's power, so raising it raises the handoff, the flyer punish, and her air clock together:

| Storm power | Buff to another attacker | Flyer punish | Swings to 21 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4 (printed) | +4 | 4 | 6 |
| 6 | +6 | 6 | 4 |
| 8 | +8 | 8 | 3 |
| 10 | +10 | 10 | 3 |

At power 4 the handoff turns a 3/3 into a 7/3 flyer; at power 8, into an 11/3 flyer. Counters, anthems that touch Storm, auras, and Equipment are the engine, because each point of power is a point of handoff, a point of air defense, and a faster commander-damage clock.

## The buff targets, who gets the handoff

The attack trigger wants a second attacker that converts a big power boost into more than one hit: trample carries the extra power past a chump block, double strike doubles the whole boosted number, and a combat-damage trigger turns the handoff into cards or removal. Since the trigger grants flying, even a ground creature works as the target. What it should not be is a vanilla body with no trample, which gives most of the boost back to a blocker. Keep a handful of trample and double-strike bodies so Storm always has a worthy second attacker.

## The flyer punish, a free removal spell

Storm's third ability deals her power to any flying creature that attacks you, a repeatable removal effect for free. Against a fliers deck it is a wall that kills on contact, and it grows with the same power boosts that fuel the offense. A Storm at power 8 clears most attacking flyers outright; a printed Storm at power 4 trades with the smaller ones. Every power boost points at both halves of the game.

## Dead Draw Audit

| Card | Primary job | Support in the 99 | Value without Storm | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Repeatable +1/+1 counter source on Storm | Raises the power that fuels all three abilities | Very high, every Storm ability | Some, grows any creature | **core** |
| Cheap trample or double-strike attacker | Turns the +X handoff into real damage | High, the attack trigger each turn | Good, an evasive beater alone | **core** |
| Protective Equipment (hexproof/indestructible) | Keeps Storm alive and adds power | High, protects the whole plan | Good on any commander | **supported** |
| Team-wide anthem | One power to Storm and the board | Medium, one point to the lever | Good, buffs the team | **supported** |
| Vanilla ground beater, no evasion | A body only | Low, wastes the handoff to a blocker | Depends on its stats | **situational** |

The vanilla beater drops to situational because it gives most of an eight-point handoff back to a blocker, where a trampler or double striker banks it. Protective Equipment climbs, since a commander that carries every buff is the first removal target, and losing Storm resets the plan.

## Beyond the Dead Draw Audit: the construction audit

Scores the whole ninety-nine on the power lever and its support.

| Target | Count | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Power boosts that touch Storm | 12-15 | Counters, auras, Equipment, anthems, the whole lever |
| Worthy buff targets | 10-12 | Trample, double strike, or combat-damage triggers |
| Protection for Storm | 5-7 | Hexproof, indestructible, protection, recast value |
| Interaction | 8-10 | Green-white removal and a reset to reach the swing |
| Ramp | 7-9 | Reach five mana and the pump suite early |
| Lands | 36-37 | A clean two-color Selesnya base |

Read the power boosts and the buff targets as one system with two halves: the boosts make the lever tall, the targets turn the handoff into damage. Report two numbers when you tune the list: power boosts that reach Storm, and trample or double-strike targets. Piling counters on Storm with no worthy second attacker wastes her best trigger; a wide creature deck with no way to grow Storm leaves her abilities small. Every revision holds the two floors and the protection count first: a new power boost ideally also protects Storm or draws a card, a new creature earns its slot as a buff target or its own evasive threat. Protection is the tax for stacking value on one body.

## The shell, a Selesnya flyers plan

Storm is a legal Wakanda Forever commander in green and white, and she wants a different ninety-nine from the artifact plans on the [T'Challa](/tchalla-black-panther-deck-tech/) and [Shuri](/shuri-black-panther-deck-tech/) pages. Where those lean on artifact count, Storm leans on creature power and evasion: a green-white midrange deck built around counters, protective Equipment, and a spine of trample and double-strike attackers, with the mana fixing and removal the two colors give. The box's monarch and Equipment sub-themes fit her well, since both keep her attacking and cards flowing. The interaction and ramp carry over from any Selesnya midrange list; only the payoff changes. Point the buffs at Storm, keep a bench of worthy targets, and protect the commander that holds the plan.
