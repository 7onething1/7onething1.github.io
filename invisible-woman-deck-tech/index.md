# Invisible Woman: The Wall That Swings

A deck tech for **Invisible Woman**, the four-color Fantastic Four Marvel Commander alt, read with Invisible Woman in the command zone. Inspired by Salubrious Snail's deckbuilding framework; oracle text from Scryfall (Marvel Super Heroes Commander, read 2026-07-14). Not authored or endorsed by Salubrious Snail or EDHREC.

**Invisible Woman** costs {2}{W}, a 3/3 Legendary Creature (Human Hero), color identity four-color (white, blue, red, green) earned through her attack ability. Two abilities: **at combat, if you cast a noncreature spell this turn, make a 0/3 colorless Wall with defender and reach**; and **whenever you attack, you may pay {R}{G}{W}{U} to give a target creature +1/+0 for each creature you control and make it unblockable**. She is a Fantastic Four alt, a four-color defensive deck that turns a wall of blockers into a single unblockable swing.

## The engine, a fortress that attacks

Invisible Woman runs a defensive plan with a sudden finish. The first ability turns noncreature spells into a growing wall of 0/3 blockers with reach, so the deck holds the ground and the air while it develops. The second spends four colors to make one creature unblockable and to add your whole creature count to its power. The trick: the Walls count toward that creature count even though defender keeps them from attacking, so the same board that keeps you alive decides how hard the finisher hits. This is a defensive combo rather than a beatdown: stall behind Walls, bank card advantage from the noncreature package, then end a player from an empty-looking board with one unblockable attacker.

## The two dials, spells and bodies

Two numbers drive the build. The first is how many noncreature spells you cast, since each makes a Wall at combat, both a blocker and a point on the finisher. The second is the total creature count, Walls included, since the buff adds one power per creature. The dials feed each other: casting noncreature spells makes Walls, and Walls raise the creature count, so a spellslinger draw engine quietly becomes a growing clock. Treat the Walls as fuel, not just defense: a stalled board is a loaded one. Report the noncreature spell count and the realistic creature count together.

## The unblockable swing

The buff adds one power per creature and makes the target unblockable, so the whole board turns into damage on one attacker nothing can stop:

| Creatures (X) | Buff | 3-power target, unblockable | Swings to 21 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | +5 | 8 | 3 |
| 8 | +8 | 11 | 2 |
| 12 | +12 | 15 | 2 |
| 18 | +18 | 21 | 1 |

At a dozen bodies, most of them Walls, a three-power attacker connects for fifteen that cannot be blocked, and a double striker doubles that into a one-shot kill. Keep making Walls, keep the board wide, and hold a cheap unblockable target or a double striker to cash the whole count in one attack.

## The four-color tax

The finisher costs one red, one green, one white, and one blue on top of your other plays, the real price of the deck. The mana base has to produce all four colors reliably every turn you want to go off, so fixing lands, four-color rocks, and mana creatures pay for the win condition and earn core slots. The upside: the payment is optional and repeatable, so you hold the four colors until the swing is lethal, then pay once and end a player. Run enough fixing that the payment is available on the turn the board is wide, and enough ramp that paying it does not cost your whole turn.

## Dead Draw Audit

| Card | Primary job | Support in the 99 | Value without Invisible Woman | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cheap noncreature cantrip | Makes a Wall and replaces itself | Very high, the Wall engine | Good, card flow anywhere | **core** |
| Four-color fixing land or rock | Pays the {R}{G}{W}{U} finisher tax | High, every go-off turn | Fixes mana in any four-color deck | **core** |
| Double striker | Doubles the unblockable swing | High, the finisher target | Good, an evasive threat alone | **supported** |
| Extra token maker | Raises the creature count for the buff | Medium, adds to X | Good, a wide board | **supported** |
| Colorless-only ramp rock | Ramp, but adds no color for the tax | Low, does not pay the finisher | Ramps in any deck | **situational** |

The colorless-only rock drops to situational because it does not help the four-color payment the whole deck is built to reach, where a fixing rock ramps and pays the tax at once. The cheap noncreature cantrips climb, since each is a Wall, a card, and a point on the finisher in one slot.

## Beyond the Dead Draw Audit: the construction audit

Scores the whole ninety-nine on the two dials and the tax.

| Target | Count | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Noncreature spells | 28-34 | Each makes a Wall, a blocker and a point on the finish |
| Extra token and creature makers | 6-10 | Raises the creature count beyond the Walls |
| Four-color fixing | heavy | Lands, rocks, and dorks that pay {R}{G}{W}{U} |
| Unblockable payoff support | 3-5 | Double strike and combat-damage triggers |
| Interaction | 8-10 | Four-color removal and a reset behind the Walls |
| Lands | 37-38 | A four-color base slanted to fixing |

Read the noncreature count and the fixing as the two things that most often fail: the Wall engine wants a noncreature spell most turns, the finisher wants four colors on demand. Report three numbers: the noncreature spell count, the realistic creature count, and the count of sources that produce each of the four colors. The honest measure is whether you can cast a spell, hold a wide wall, and pay {R}{G}{W}{U} on the same turn. Every revision holds the noncreature floor and the fixing first: a new noncreature spell ideally draws a card or interacts, and a new mana source adds a color the finisher needs rather than colorless ramp. The Walls are free defense, so the deck spends its slots on spells, fixing, and one reliable finisher target.

## The shell, a four-color Fantastic Four plan

Invisible Woman shares the four-color Fantastic Four base on the [Mister Fantastic deck tech](/mister-fantastic-deck-tech/), since both reward casting noncreature spells, so the fixing and much of the spell package carry over. Where [Crystal](/crystal-inhuman-princess-deck-tech/) turns those spells into direct damage and Mister Fantastic doubles their triggers, Invisible Woman turns them into Walls and a single unblockable swing. Under her the shell leans more defensive: cheap interactive noncreature spells, a heavier fixing base for the {R}{G}{W}{U} tax, and one or two double strikers to cash the creature count. The interaction and card draw carry over from any four-color spellslinger list; the build changes the payoff and the mana.
