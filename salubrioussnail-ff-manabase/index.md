# The Fantastic Four's Manabase, Built My Way

The only four-color deck in the Marvel Super Heroes Commander box has one real tax, and it is color. This is my whole-manabase video worked on the actual precon, instead of a link to it.

## Two jobs, one set of lands

A land base covers two goals at once. The first is covering your symbols: lands make the colors you need to cast your spells. The second is covering total mana values, which mostly means hitting your land drops so the deck keeps climbing its curve. Utility lands live in the gap between the two. For the Fantastic Four, hitting drops is the easy goal. The hard goal is color, because four colors is a lot of colors, and this deck wants them early and often.

## Which colors, and when

Covering symbols depends on three things at once: how many of each color you run, how intense each requirement is, and how early the deck needs it. Blue and white are the early asks (Mister Fantastic {2}{U}, Invisible Woman {2}{W}). Human Torch {3}{R} comes a turn later. The Thing {5}{G} is the latest ask, so a stray green source on turn five is fine. The sharpest requirement belongs to Mister Fantastic's activated ability, {R}{G}{W}{U}, which wants one of every color at once. Build the mana so that button is castable, and the rest follows.

## Is the trouble fixing, or too few lands?

I built a free manabase tool with my brother Ian that scores a deck by cast rate and average delay, then runs a test: add one basic of each color, add one colorless "waste," and see which helps. If the waste helps a lot, the deck needs more lands. If a colored basic helps far more, the deck needs that color. Counting sources for this precon gives sixteen white, sixteen blue, fourteen red, and seventeen green, so no color is starved. The real tax is hitting two or three colors at once early, which cheap untapped fixing smooths, and the two Talismans do exactly that. The three swaps below carry it out. For exact cast-rate and average-delay figures, run the list through the tool.

## The three swaps

- **Willie Lumpkin, Postman → Talisman of Creativity.** A flavor legend for a mana rock that taps blue or red on turn two, a turn ahead of your three-mana commander.
- **Mirage Mirror → Talisman of Conviction.** A slow toolbox for a second fixing rock (white or red). Two Talismans is the deck telling you its problem is color. Together they reach three of the four colors and double up on red.
- **Into the Time Vortex → West Coast Expansion.** A card-flow upgrade once the two Talismans handle color. Into the Time Vortex is a cascade and rebound spell that runs feast or famine; West Coast Expansion draws X, and at X of five or more it casts a Hero for free. Want a third fixing piece instead? Use a Fellwar Stone or another Talisman.

## The budget trap: tapped lands

A land that enters tapped is a turn you did not act. This deck wants a noncreature spell down every turn to keep all four faces awake, so tapped budget duals on turns two and three cost it its best windows. Watch how often each dual actually enters untapped before you add it.

## Your call: how safe do you want to feel

There is no single correct land count; it depends on how much mana risk you enjoy. The Fantastic Four belongs on the safe end for most tables, because its ceiling only shows up once the four colors are online. Pay the fixing tax in full, and the ceiling takes care of itself.
