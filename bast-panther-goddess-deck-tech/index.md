# Bast, Panther Goddess: The Width Payoff

A deck tech for **Bast, Panther Goddess**, the Selesnya Wakanda Forever alt, read with Bast in the command zone. Inspired by Salubrious Snail's deckbuilding framework; oracle text from Scryfall (Marvel Super Heroes Commander, read 2026-07-14). Not authored or endorsed by Salubrious Snail or EDHREC.

**Bast, Panther Goddess** costs {2}{G}{W}, a 4/4 Legendary Creature (Cat God), Selesnya, with **reach, trample, indestructible**. She **cannot attack or block unless you control three or more creatures**, and **whenever you attack, a target attacking creature gets +X/+X, where X is the number of creatures you control**. She is a Wakanda Forever alt, a green-white go-wide deck that turns a full board into a single lethal swing. Her indestructible body makes her the safest home for her own buff, so a wide board plus Bast is a resilient, hard-to-block finisher.

## The engine, wide equals damage

Bast reads your creature count and turns it into combat damage. The buff is +X/+X where X is your creature count, so every extra body raises the swing. Point it at Bast herself and she gains her whole board as power on an indestructible trampler that cannot be chump-blocked away. The three-creature clause is the price: Bast does nothing on an empty board. She is a payoff rather than an enabler. She cashes the board rather than filling it, so the ninety-nine carries the go-wide half and Bast supplies the finish.

## The width lever

X is your creature count, so the whole build points at one number, how wide the board is when Bast swings (buff aimed at Bast, the most durable target):

| Creatures (X) | Buff | Bast buffed | Swings to 21 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 (the gate) | +3 | 7/7 | 3 |
| 5 | +5 | 9/9 | 3 |
| 7 | +7 | 11/11 | 2 |
| 10 | +10 | 14/14 | 2 |
| 17 | +17 | 21/21 | 1 |

Each body is a point of buff, and because Bast tramples and cannot be destroyed, that buff turns into face damage rather than a trade. Token makers, cheap creatures, and anything that leaves two or more bodies are the engine. Anthems add to the same swing by raising the base of every attacker.

## The three-creature gate

Bast cannot attack or block below three creatures, a real construction requirement. Run enough cheap early creatures and token makers that three bodies are on the table by the time Bast lands, or she sits inert as a 4/4 that cannot even block. That argues for a low creature curve, a healthy count of one and two-drops, and token makers that produce two or more at once so a single card clears the gate. The gate also shapes recovery: a board wipe drops you under three and switches Bast off even though she survives it, so run token producers that rebuild width fast rather than one big threat a wipe leaves stranded. Width is the payoff and the on-switch at once.

## The buff targets, Bast first

The trigger targets any attacking creature, and Bast is usually the best home: indestructible, so damage and destruction removal does not answer the boosted threat, though exile, bounce, and sacrifice still can, and trample, so blockers cannot eat the surplus. A trampling or double-striking attacker is the backup when Bast is held to block, and a combat-damage trigger turns the +X/+X into extra value. Avoid a fragile body with no evasion, since a chump block wastes a plus ten swing.

## Dead Draw Audit

| Card | Primary job | Support in the 99 | Value without Bast | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Token maker leaving two or more bodies | Clears the gate and raises X | Very high, every Bast swing | Good, a wide board alone | **core** |
| Repeatable token engine | Rebuilds width after a wipe | High, the recovery plan | Good, ongoing value | **core** |
| Team-wide anthem | Adds base power to every attacker | High, scales with width | Good, buffs the team | **supported** |
| Cheap one-drop creature | Helps clear the gate early | Medium, matters before turn four | Low, a small body alone | **supported** |
| Single big threat with no board | One body, no width | Low, does not raise X or clear the gate | Depends on its stats | **situational** |

The lone big threat drops to situational because it adds one to X and nothing to resilience, where a token maker adds several and helps clear the gate. The repeatable token engine climbs, since the deck's weakness is a wipe that switches Bast off, and a card that rebuilds three bodies turns her back on.

## Beyond the Dead Draw Audit: the construction audit

Scores the whole ninety-nine on the width lever and its gate.

| Target | Count | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Creatures and token makers | 32-38 | Width is the payoff and the on-switch |
| Cards that leave two or more bodies | 10-14 | Clears the gate and rebuilds after a wipe |
| Early development cards | 12-16 | Three bodies before Bast: cheap creatures, mana dorks, token spells, artifact creatures, and body-making enchantments |
| Anthems and go-wide payoffs | 4-6 | Raises the base of every attacker |
| Interaction | 8-10 | Green-white removal and a reset of your own |
| Card advantage | 6-8 | Refills the hand so the board keeps rebuilding after a wipe |
| Protection for Bast and the board | 3-5 | Answers the exile, bounce, and sacrifice that indestructible does not stop |
| Lands (mana sources) | 36-38 | A clean Selesnya base |
| Ramp | 6-8 | Early acceleration to reach the gate and the payoff sooner |

Read the creature count and the multi-body count as one system: the creature count sets the buff size, the multi-body count controls how fast you clear the gate and recover from a wipe. Report two numbers when you tune the list: the total creature count and the count of cards that leave two or more bodies. The honest measure is the board width you can reach and rebuild, beyond the raw creature slots on paper. Every revision holds the width floor and the multi-body count first: a token maker that leaves several bodies beats a single large body of the same cost. Bast shrugs off damage and destruction removal, so the deck spends fewer slots shielding the commander than a fragile one would, though it still wants a few answers to the exile, bounce, and sacrifice that get past indestructible.

## The shell, a Selesnya go-wide plan

Bast wants a different ninety-nine from the other Wakanda faces. The [T'Challa](/tchalla-black-panther-deck-tech/) and [Shuri](/shuri-black-panther-deck-tech/) plans count artifacts, and [Storm](/storm-queen-wakanda-deck-tech/) stacks power on one commander. Bast counts creatures, so the shell is a green-white tokens and go-wide deck: token makers, cheap creatures, a few anthems, and the mana and removal the two colors give. The box's own token and monarch pieces fit her, since both reward a developed board. The interaction and ramp carry over from any Selesnya go-wide list; only the payoff changes. Fill the board, clear the three-creature gate early, and point the buff at the indestructible goddess, who shrugs off damage and destruction removal even when exile, bounce, or sacrifice can still answer her.
