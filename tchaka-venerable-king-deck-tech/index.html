T'Chaka, Venerable King costs {G}{W}, a 2/2 Selesnya Hero with two abilities. When he enters, mill three cards, then you may take an artifact or land card from among them into your hand. And for three mana, exiling him from your graveyard, you become the monarch, but only if you control your commander. He is a Wakanda Forever alt, and EDHREC shows him thinly built, which the card explains on its own. The enter trigger is a small, repeatable dig for ramp and fixing, and the whole deck is a plan to trigger it again and again through blink and recursion.

T'Chaka is a 2/2 that costs two mana. When he enters, he mills three cards and you can take an artifact or land. His second power makes you the monarch, but it needs you to control a commander, which rarely happens when he is your commander. So the deck is built around blinking him to reuse the first power.

The engine, a repeatable dig

The enter trigger is the reason to build T'Chaka. It mills three and hands you an artifact or land from the three, which is ramp, fixing, and a little graveyard filling in one cheap package. On its own it is minor, one card that is usually a land. Repeated, it becomes a real engine, since a two-mana commander that digs every time he enters rewards any way to flicker or recast him. The deck turns a small trigger into a steady stream of lands, mana rocks, and graveyard fuel.

The build is a Selesnya value deck built on that loop. Blink effects return T'Chaka to the battlefield for another dig, sacrifice-and-recur lines reuse him, and his low cost keeps the command-zone tax cheap when he dies. None of this is explosive, and all of it stays consistent, which is the honest pitch for a commander the community has mostly passed over.

The first power is small but repeatable. Each time T'Chaka enters, you dig for a land or artifact. Blink him or bring him back to do it over and over, and those little digs add up to steady ramp and card selection. He is consistent rather than flashy.

The honest read on the monarch ability

The second ability deserves a straight answer rather than a sales pitch. It reads: pay three, exile T'Chaka from your graveyard, and you become the monarch, only if you control your commander. When T'Chaka is your sole commander, he goes to the command zone rather than your graveyard, and he is not on the battlefield for you to control, so the ability has almost no window to work. It was written for a deck where T'Chaka sits in the ninety-nine beside a different commander, or one that deliberately routes him to the graveyard.

So the page grades that ability near zero for a T'Chaka-led deck and builds around the enter trigger instead. A pilot who wants the monarch line should run T'Chaka in the ninety-nine under another Selesnya commander, where controlling that commander turns the ability on. Saying this plainly is the point, since dressing a dead ability up as an engine is how a primer misleads a new pilot.

The monarch power needs you to control a commander. If T'Chaka is your commander, he is in the command zone or graveyard instead of on the battlefield, so you do not control him and the power does nothing. It only works if T'Chaka is a normal card in a deck led by someone else. We are honest about that and build around his first power.

The dig math

The enter trigger mills three and takes an artifact or land, so its reliability depends on how many lands and artifacts the deck runs. At a normal Selesnya land count the grab almost always finds a target, and adding a few artifacts pushes it higher. Read the right column as how often a dig gives you a card. The takeaway is to keep the land count healthy and add a modest artifact package, since both raise the hit rate and both are valid grabs. A deck that tilts its nonland slots toward artifacts turns the dig into repeatable ramp, and one that stays land-heavy turns it into fixing and land drops. Either way the trigger rarely whiffs, which is what makes the blink loop worth building.

The dig looks at three cards and takes a land or artifact, so it works better with more lands and artifacts. At a normal land count it finds a card about three times in four, and adding artifacts pushes it higher. So run plenty of lands and some artifacts.

The blink package

Since the enter trigger is the engine, the build wants a real package of ways to reuse it. Blink and flicker effects return T'Chaka for another dig at instant speed, sacrifice outlets paired with recursion recycle him for value, and cheap reanimation brings him back after a wipe. Green and white both offer this, from flicker spells and blink creatures to populate-adjacent recursion, so the package fits the colors.

The milled cards are a second, quiet resource. Every dig bins two cards you did not take, so a light graveyard payoff turns the mill into extra value: recursion that returns creatures or spells, a card that rewards a stocked graveyard, or landfall that pays off the lands you grab. None of this needs to be heavy, since the deck is a value grind, and a couple of payoffs keep the milled cards from being wasted.

Run blink and flicker cards to make T'Chaka enter again and again, plus a way to bring him back if he dies. Also add a few cards that use your graveyard, since every dig mills two cards you do not keep. A little graveyard payoff turns that into extra value.

Read the blink count and the land-and-artifact count as one system. The blink count sets how often you repeat the dig, and the land-and-artifact count sets how often the dig gives you a card, so a deck with blink and a thin mana base whiffs its loop and a land-heavy deck with no blink digs only once. Report two numbers when you tune the list, the count of ways to reuse T'Chaka and the count of lands plus artifacts. The honest measure is how many free digs you get across a game and how many find a card, since that is the entire engine, and the deck needs a separate plan to actually win.

Every revision should hold the blink count and the mana base first, then a payoff for the milled cards, then a real finishing plan, since T'Chaka is a two-power body that does not end games. A new blink effect that also protects or draws is worth more than a narrow one. A card that only supports the monarch line is the first cut for a T'Chaka-led build. The shell, a Selesnya value plan

T'Chaka is a legal Wakanda Forever commander in green and white, and he wants the most modest build of the Wakanda faces. The T'Challa and Shuri plans count artifacts, Storm stacks power, Bast goes wide, and Okoye dances the crown. T'Chaka just grinds value from a repeated dig. The shell is a green-white blink and midrange deck with a healthy mana base, a light graveyard payoff, and a real creature plan to close, since the commander supplies consistency rather than a finish.

The interaction and the ramp carry over from any Selesnya midrange list, and the ramp doubles as dig fuel. Reuse the enter trigger, keep the mana base high so it hits, and win with the creatures and payoffs the two colors provide. Build toward the construction audit above, a real blink package, a deep mana base, a couple of graveyard payoffs, and an honest finishing plan, with no weight spent on the monarch ability that does not work under him.

T'Chaka wants the simplest of the Wakanda decks: green-white blink and good creatures. The other faces have flashy plans; he just grinds steady value. Reuse his dig, keep lots of lands, and win with normal creatures, since he does not finish games himself.
