# Shuri, the Black Panther: The Artifact-Threshold Machine

A construction audit for **Shuri, the Black Panther**, the Wakanda Forever Marvel Commander alt, read with Shuri in the command zone. Inspired by Salubrious Snail's deckbuilding framework; card text from Scryfall. Not authored or endorsed by Salubrious Snail or EDHREC.

Shuri reads as one attack trigger with two gates. Control three artifacts when she swings and you draw a card. Control six and every creature you have gets plus two power and toughness for the turn. This is a Selesnya artifact deck that turns a rising artifact count into cards early and a team beatdown late. The build question: how reliably you reach three, how often you reach six, and how many creatures are standing to use the bonus.

## Beyond the Dead Draw Audit: the construction audit

The Dead Draw Audit scores individual cards against their support. This construction audit steps up one level and scores the whole 99. It asks whether the complete list reaches both thresholds while developing enough creatures to use the attack bonus. Shuri leads here, so the ninety-nine must reach both numbers on its own. Effects that need T'Challa producing Vibranium first receive a lower grade, and independent producers like Shuri's Fabricator rise in value. This extends Snail's dead-draw, fulcrum-card, support-strength, and independent-route methods to the level of the full list.

## Artifact threshold accounting

The stock Wakanda Forever list carries twenty-four cards in its dedicated artifact category, plus four artifact creatures (Metalwork Colossus, Meteor Golem, Panther Robot, Solemn Simulacrum); The Great Mound may sort differently by its printed type line. Generate one exact artifact total from the real manifest rather than a round number, then separate the artifacts by cost and job: two mana or less, artifact creatures, token producers, mana artifacts, Equipment, Vehicles, and expensive value pieces. A deck of thirty artifacts can still stall when most cost five or more.

**Suggested Shuri adds:** Tree of Tales, Ancient Den, Darksteel Citadel, Thornglint Bridge, Foundry Inspector, Retrofitter Foundry, Illustrious Wanderglyph, Thousand Moons Smithy, Haywire Mite, Stridehangar Automaton. Each is an artifact card or artifact mana source. Publish the new artifact total after every completed exchange.

## Three artifacts: the draw gate

Shuri needs three artifacts in play when the trigger resolves. About twenty-eight artifact cards give roughly a **57.8 percent** chance of three or more among the first ten library cards (63.6 percent at thirty). That measures access, not deployment, so expensive or removed artifacts can drop the live count below the number seen.

- Target at least **twelve artifacts costing zero, one, or two mana** (mana artifacts, cheap Equipment, Haywire Mite, Retrofitter Foundry), several of which also give mana, interaction, selection, or tokens.
- Pair cheap artifacts with **token production**: Treasure, Clue, Food, Thopter, Construct, and Vibranium tokens satisfy Shuri while doing a second job, and a card making several artifacts repairs a wiped board faster than another standalone artifact.

## Six artifacts: the team pump

Six-plus artifacts in an early sample is far rarer, about **2.8 percent** at twenty-eight artifacts, so the deck needs cards that make several artifacts from one slot: Retrofitter Foundry, Thousand Moons Smithy, Shuri's Fabricator, Royal Talon Fighter Jet, token-making creatures. Distinguish permanent artifact cards from production capacity: twenty-eight artifacts plus eight repeatable token sources reaches six differently from thirty-six standalone artifacts, and rebuilds better after a wipe. Because Shuri leads, six must be reachable without T'Challa making Vibranium, which is why independent producers like Shuri's Fabricator matter more here.

## Fulcrum cards and artifact creatures

Shuri's six-artifact bonus pumps creatures, so **artifact creatures** satisfy the threshold and collect the plus two, making them the strongest card type for her plan. Field **twelve to sixteen** of them (Metalwork Colossus, Meteor Golem, Panther Robot, Solemn Simulacrum, Foundry Inspector, Haywire Mite, Illustrious Wanderglyph, Stridehangar Automaton). Protect the fulcrum cards during cuts, since each links several jobs: Illustrious Wanderglyph, Thousand Moons Smithy, Retrofitter Foundry, Shuri's Fabricator, Panther Robot, Royal Talon Fighter Jet, Foundry Inspector.

**Creature width.** The attack bonus grows with the creature count: one big creature gains two power, six creatures gain twelve. Publish the number of cards that make two or more creatures or tokens (Martial Coup, Royal Talon Fighter Jet, Retrofitter Foundry, Thousand Moons Smithy, Illustrious Wanderglyph, Stridehangar Automaton). Divine Visitation earns its slot only after enough token makers exist; its value follows the verified token count.

## Cost, curve, and the draw suite

Foundry Inspector cuts generic artifact costs, helping push a board from three toward six in one turn; calculate how many artifacts actually gain (mana artifacts none, colored-cost little, expensive generic the most). Shuri is two mana, so early acceleration should build artifacts and width; Sol Ring, Arcane Signet, and Thran Dynamo satisfy the threshold and add resources. Count every nonland card costing five or more, and swap new expensive cards for existing ones so the curve cannot creep. Shuri draws one card per qualifying attack, so keep independent draw for combat-hard games, separated by job: immediate (Harmonize), continuing (Mind's Eye), artifact selection (Ingenious Smith), and recovery. A draw effect that itself needs six artifacts only repeats the commander's condition.

## Interaction, protection, and recovery

Some answers ride inside the engine: Haywire Mite (artifact/enchantment removal plus count), Meteor Golem (broad removal on a body), Scourglass, King Solomon's Frogs. **Dispatch** is the sharpest overlap: three artifacts turn on metalcraft to exile rather than tap, the same number that fires Shuri's first reward. Keep cheap colored interaction and full coverage (creature, artifact, enchantment, graveyard, wide board) rather than trading Beast Within or Generous Gift for artifact-only answers. Shuri must attack, so keep creature protection, haste, and evasion (Hammer of Nazahn, Whispersilk Cloak, noting its shroud blocks your own targeting) and measure protection, recursion, and token-rebuilding as three separate counts (Trading Post, Shuri's Fabricator, Conduit of Worlds, Thousand Moons Smithy, Retrofitter Foundry).

## Commander independence and the mana base

Keep the artifact-creature plan working when Shuri is gone: the artifact count still powers Dispatch, Ingenious Smith, Metalwork Colossus, and the value engines. Give every addition an independence score (full, partial, commander-dependent). Artifact lands (Tree of Tales, Ancient Den, Darksteel Citadel, Thornglint Bridge) raise density without spending nonland slots; record artifact type, colored access, and tapped status, and protect enough untapped green and white for Shuri and early interaction.

## Finish density and upgrade guardrails

Shuri turns six artifacts and a wide board into a repeated team bonus, so the deck needs fewer dedicated finishers; creatures with trample, flying, or menace gain the most, and team evasion can beat another team pump (Stridehangar Automaton, Illustrious Wanderglyph, Thousand Moons Smithy, Panther Robot, Royal Talon Fighter Jet).

Every revision should hold: a verified artifact total from the manifest, at least twelve cheap artifacts, a substantial group of artifact creatures, several repeatable token makers, and enough creatures to convert the bonus. Swap like for like (artifact for artifact, expensive for expensive, artifact land for comparable land, token maker for weaker token maker). Record each addition's effect on artifact cards, artifact creatures, token capacity, creature count, curve, and colored access, and give it one primary job. The finished hundred should reach three artifacts easily, six repeatedly, and field enough creatures to make the plus two land.
