# Master Prompt — Build a World-Class Website for *Emotional Alchemy*

> A complete, copy-paste-ready build brief for an AI website builder (Claude Code,
> v0, Lovable, Cursor) **or** a human designer/developer. It turns the *Emotional
> Alchemy — 5 Stages* framework into a calm, luxurious, deeply intentional site.
> Everything below is the prompt: paste all of it, or start from the **TL;DR
> Prompt** and pull in the sections you need.

---

## TL;DR Prompt (paste this to start)

> You are a senior brand designer and front-end engineer building a world-class
> website for **Emotional Alchemy**, a practice that helps people turn emotional
> pain into power and freedom. The site's spine is a **5-stage journey** —
> *Identify the Pain (Fire) → Trace to Origin (Water) → Acceptance & De-Shaming
> (Air) → Transmutation (Earth) → Transformation (Rubedo/Circle)*. Design it to
> feel like a **guided descent and return**: quiet, spacious, sacred, and
> emotionally safe — the opposite of a loud SaaS landing page.
>
> Use thin alchemical line-symbols (never filled), an earthy apothecary palette
> (forest green, plum, blush, sage, bone, champagne, soft gold), generous negative
> space, a refined editorial serif paired with a humanist sans, and slow,
> breath-paced motion. Build a scroll-driven experience where each stage's symbol
> **draws itself in** as the reader arrives. Copy must be de-shaming and sovereign
> — *"Nothing is wrong with you."*
>
> Ship a fully responsive, WCAG-AA accessible, Lighthouse 95+ static site with
> semantic HTML, respects `prefers-reduced-motion`, and loads fast. Deliver: Home,
> The Journey (5 stages), About / The Practitioner, Work With Me (offerings),
> Reflections (journal), and Contact. Follow the full brand + design system spec
> below to the letter.

---

## 1. Role & Objective

**You are:** a principal-level brand designer *and* front-end engineer with a
portfolio of award-winning wellness, therapy, and slow-luxury brands (think
Aesop, Maude, Headspace's quieter cousin, boutique somatic-therapy studios).

**Your objective:** design and build the marketing/experience website for
**Emotional Alchemy** — a modality that helps people *turn pain into power and
freedom*. The website is not a brochure; it is the first felt experience of the
work. A visitor should leave calmer than they arrived, and quietly certain that
this practitioner understands them.

**North-star test:** if you removed all the text, the *layout, pacing, color, and
motion alone* should still communicate "safe," "spacious," "sacred," and
"grounded." Loud, salesy, cramped, or clinical = failure.

---

## 2. Brand Foundation

- **Name:** Emotional Alchemy
- **One-line promise:** *Turning pain into power and freedom.*
- **What it is:** a 5-stage emotional-transformation process using alchemical
  symbolism (Fire, Water, Air, Earth, Rubedo) as a map for moving through
  triggers, memory, shame, integration, and renewal.
- **Audience:** thoughtful adults doing inner work — people who are done with
  quick fixes and hustle-culture healing; who value depth, safety, and beauty.
- **Emotional promise:** *You are not broken. You can feel this safely. Nothing is
  wrong with you. You are whole — and free.*
- **Brand personality:** grounded · reverent · warm · precise · unhurried ·
  quietly luxurious. A wise, non-judgmental guide — never a guru, never a coach
  shouting hacks.
- **Anti-patterns (avoid entirely):** neon gradients, stock "happy people jumping"
  photography, exclamation-heavy hype, countdown timers, aggressive pop-ups,
  clinical/medical sterility, new-age cliché (crystals-and-glitter kitsch).

---

## 3. The Core Narrative — The 5 Stages

This framework **is the information architecture**. The main experience page walks
the visitor through all five, in order, as a descent-and-return arc. Each stage
has a symbol, a meaning, an emotional beat, a one-line "voice," and a precise
visual treatment. Honor the visual notes exactly.

| # | Stage | Symbol | Element | The felt moment | Voice |
|---|-------|--------|---------|-----------------|-------|
| 1 | **Identify the Pain** | Upward triangle ▲ | **Fire** | Awareness. Activation. "Something's here." The trigger, the discomfort. Fire is *information, not chaos.* | *"Pay attention. This matters."* |
| 2 | **Trace to Origin** | Downward triangle ▽ | **Water** | Feeling, memory, depth. Tracing the emotion backward to the first imprint; the body remembering. A *gentle descent, not drowning.* | *"You can feel this safely."* |
| 3 | **Acceptance & De-Shaming** | Upward triangle with a horizontal line ⟁ | **Air** | Perspective, compassion, witnessing without blame. Shame releases; innocence returns. | *"Nothing is wrong with you."* |
| 4 | **Transmutation** | Downward triangle with a line ⟇ | **Earth** | Integration, regulation, embodiment. Insight becomes lived; the nervous system settles; choice replaces reaction. *The alchemy itself.* | *"Let this land in the body."* |
| 5 | **Transformation** | Circle ◯ (Rubedo / wholeness) | **Rubedo** | Authenticity, emotional sovereignty, love embodied. Living differently; the pattern no longer repeats. *Completion without closure — ongoing life.* | *"You are whole — and free."* |

**Per-stage visual treatment (bind these to the design system in §5):**

1. **Fire —** thin linework; **forest green or plum** stroke; symbol *never filled*.
2. **Water —** slightly softened edges; **blush or sage** tones; pair with open space.
3. **Air —** very light stroke; **bone or champagne** tones; the most generous negative space of any stage.
4. **Earth —** grounded placement (lower in the viewport, weightier); **forest green dominant**; pair with texture (stone, linen).
5. **Rubedo —** **soft gold or muted blush**; never heavy; the circle often used as an *enclosing* frame around content.

**Arc of the page:** color and light should *descend* into Water/Earth (deeper,
cooler, more textured) and *rise* into Air and Rubedo (lighter, warmer, gilded).
The reader should physically feel the journey down and back up.

## 4. Sitemap & Page Requirements

Keep it small, intentional, and complete. Six destinations:

1. **Home** — the invitation.
   - Hero: the promise (*"Turn pain into power and freedom."*), one grounding
     sub-line, one calm primary CTA (*"Begin the journey"* → The Journey; a quiet
     secondary CTA *"Work with me"*). No carousel, no hype.
   - A short "What is Emotional Alchemy?" band (2–3 sentences, lots of air).
   - A preview of the 5 stages as five line-symbols in a row that draw in on
     scroll and link into the full Journey page.
   - A single, honest testimonial or ethos statement.
   - Soft closing CTA with the Rubedo circle enclosing it.

2. **The Journey** — the centerpiece. A scroll-driven, one-stage-at-a-time
   experience through all five stages (see §3 and §6). This is where the craft
   shows. Each stage is a full "chapter": symbol drawing in, meaning, the
   bulleted felt-states, and the stage's one-line voice set large and quiet.

3. **About / The Practitioner** — story, philosophy, credentials or lineage,
   why this work, and the safety ethos. Warm portrait treatment (duotone in brand
   tones, never a cold headshot). Establish trust and humanity.

4. **Work With Me** — offerings (sessions, programs, or workshops). Each offering
   as a calm card: what it is, who it's for, the shape of it, gentle pricing or
   "inquire." Clear but unpushy CTAs. An FAQ that pre-answers fear ("Is this
   therapy?", "Is it safe?", "What if I've tried everything?").

5. **Reflections** — a journal/blog index for essays and practices. Editorial,
   readable, generous line-length and leading. (Structure it so posts are easy to
   add later.)

6. **Contact** — a short, human form (name, email, a single open "What's present
   for you?" field), plus a direct email and expectation-setting on response time.
   Privacy reassurance. Optional gentle newsletter opt-in (*"Occasional
   reflections. No noise. Unsubscribe anytime."*).

**Global:** a quiet sticky header that fades in on scroll-up; a grounded footer
with the five symbols, nav, contact, a one-line mission, and legal/privacy.
Include a **crisis-safety note** in the footer (*"Emotional Alchemy is not a
substitute for emergency or clinical care"* + a link/line to appropriate
resources) — this is an ethical requirement for this category.

## 5. Design System

### 5.1 Color — earthy apothecary palette
Use these as design tokens. Warm, muted, natural; nothing saturated or neon.

| Token | Role | Hex (starting point — tune for contrast) |
|-------|------|------|
| `--forest-900` | Deepest ground / Earth, text on light | `#1F2C24` |
| `--forest-700` | Primary brand green | `#2E4034` |
| `--plum-600` | Fire accent, links | `#6B4B5E` |
| `--sage-400` | Water calm | `#9BAA8E` |
| `--blush-300` | Water soft / Rubedo muted | `#E3C9C2` |
| `--champagne-200` | Air light | `#EADCC0` |
| `--bone-100` | Base background | `#F4EDE1` |
| `--bone-050` | Card / raised surface | `#FBF6EC` |
| `--gold-500` | Rubedo, sparing highlights only | `#C4A24C` |
| `--ink-900` | Body text | `#221E1A` |

Rules: **bone is the default canvas**; forest green carries weight and grounds
lower sections; gold is a *seasoning*, never a fill for large areas; the Journey
page shifts its dominant token per stage (Fire→plum/forest, Water→sage/blush,
Air→champagne/bone, Earth→forest, Rubedo→gold/blush). Ship a tasteful **dark
mode** (deep forest canvas, bone text, gold accents) via `prefers-color-scheme`.

### 5.2 Typography
- **Display / headings:** a refined, literary serif with soft optical sizing —
  **Fraunces** (variable) or **Cormorant Garamond**. Large, airy, low-contrast
  weights. Headlines can be near-whispered (thin/light weights at large sizes).
- **Body:** a warm humanist sans — **Inter**, **Instrument Sans**, or
  **Söhne**-like. Comfortable size (17–19px base), generous leading (1.6–1.75),
  measure capped ~66ch for reading passages.
- **Accent / stage labels:** let-spaced small caps of the serif or sans for
  "Stage 01 — Fire" eyebrows.
- Self-host fonts (or use `font-display: swap`); no layout shift.

### 5.3 The alchemical symbols (the visual signature)
Build the five marks as **clean inline SVGs**, single-stroke, *never filled*,
using `currentColor` so they inherit stage color:
- Fire — upward triangle. Water — downward triangle. Air — upward triangle with a
  horizontal bar. Earth — downward triangle with a horizontal bar. Rubedo — a
  circle (usable as a large enclosing frame).
- Stroke weight thin and consistent (≈1.25–1.75px optical). Perfect geometry.
- Animate with `stroke-dasharray`/`stroke-dashoffset` so each symbol **draws
  itself** when it scrolls into view (see §6).

### 5.4 Space, layout & texture
- **Negative space is a feature, not a gap.** Be generous — especially at Stage 3
  (Air). Let sections breathe with large vertical rhythm.
- Asymmetric, editorial layouts over rigid centered blocks; a fine baseline grid.
- Subtle **paper/linen/stone texture** on grounded (Earth) sections only — barely
  perceptible grain, never busy. Everything else stays clean.
- Rounded-but-restrained radii; hairline dividers in low-contrast tones; soft,
  diffuse shadows (or none). No hard borders, no drop-shadow gloss.

### 5.5 Imagery
Duotone/monotone in brand tones; natural textures (stone, water, linen, smoke,
light through leaves), abstract over literal. **No stocky lifestyle photos.**
Optional slow, muted background video/gradient only if it never distracts.

## 6. Motion & Interaction (breath-paced)

Motion should feel like **breathing**, not bouncing. Everything slow, eased, and
purposeful.

- **Symbol draw-in:** as each stage enters the viewport, its SVG strokes in over
  ~1.2–1.8s with a gentle ease; meaning + text fade/rise ~200ms after.
- **Scroll reveals:** low-amplitude fade-and-rise (opacity 0→1, translateY
  12–16px), staggered; never parallax-heavy or janky.
- **Journey pacing:** consider a soft scroll-progress indicator drawn as the five
  symbols filling in as you descend — orienting, not gamified.
- **Micro-interactions:** links and buttons transition color over 200–300ms; a
  faint underline that draws in; buttons feel like a calm press, no big scale pops.
- **Ambient life (optional, tasteful):** a barely-there breathing pulse on the
  Rubedo circle (scale 1→1.02 over ~6s), or a slow light shift in the hero.
- **Reduced motion:** fully honor `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` — symbols
  appear already-drawn, reveals become instant/opacity-only, no ambient loops.
  The site must be complete and beautiful with motion off.

---

## 7. Voice & Copy Guidelines

- **De-shaming and sovereign.** The reader is whole, capable, and safe. Never
  pathologize. Reflect the five voices: *"Pay attention. This matters." · "You can
  feel this safely." · "Nothing is wrong with you." · "Let this land in the body."
  · "You are whole — and free."*
- **Calm and spacious.** Short sentences. Room to breathe. No hype, no urgency, no
  fear-based marketing.
- **Warm, not clinical; grounded, not woo.** Precise and human. Use the alchemy
  metaphor with restraint and clarity, not mysticism for its own sake.
- **Second person, present tense.** Speak *to* the reader, gently.
- Where copy is unknown, write tasteful, on-brand **placeholder copy** in this
  voice (clearly marked) rather than lorem ipsum.

---

## 8. Technical Requirements

- **Stack:** prefer a clean static build — semantic HTML5 + modern CSS
  (custom properties, `clamp()` fluid type, container/`@media` queries, grid) +
  a little vanilla JS or a light framework (Astro/11ty/Next static export). It
  must deploy to **static hosting / GitHub Pages** with no server dependency.
- **Responsive:** mobile-first; flawless from 360px to ultrawide. Touch targets
  ≥44px; the Journey experience must degrade gracefully to a clean vertical read
  on mobile.
- **Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA, non-negotiable):** semantic landmarks, logical
  heading order, visible focus states, keyboard-operable everything, alt text,
  `aria` only where needed, AA+ contrast on all text (verify the muted palette
  passes — darken tokens as needed), `prefers-reduced-motion` respected, forms
  fully labeled with clear error states.
- **Performance:** Lighthouse **95+** across Performance/Accessibility/Best
  Practices/SEO. Optimized/lazy images (AVIF/WebP), no CLS, inline critical CSS,
  defer non-critical JS, self-hosted subset fonts. Fast on mid-range mobile.
- **SEO & sharing:** unique titles/meta descriptions, Open Graph + Twitter cards,
  favicon set, `sitemap.xml`, `robots.txt`, JSON-LD (`Organization` / `Person` /
  `Service`), sensible clean URLs.
- **Quality:** clean, commented, componentized code; design tokens centralized;
  no console errors; cross-browser (latest Chrome/Safari/Firefox/Edge). Provide a
  short README on structure, tokens, and how to add a Reflections post.

## 9. Deliverables

1. The full responsive site: **Home, The Journey, About, Work With Me,
   Reflections (index + one sample post), Contact.**
2. A centralized **design-token** file (CSS custom properties) and the five
   symbol SVGs as reusable components.
3. Working **breath-paced motion** with a reduced-motion fallback.
4. README: structure, tokens, fonts, how to extend.
5. All acceptance criteria in §10 met.

---

## 10. Acceptance Criteria (Definition of Done)

- [ ] The **5-stage journey** is the site's spine and reads as a felt
      descent-and-return, in the correct order, with each stage's exact color +
      symbol treatment from §3.
- [ ] Each alchemical symbol is a **thin, unfilled** inline SVG that draws itself
      in on scroll (and appears drawn under reduced motion).
- [ ] Palette, type, spacing, and texture match §5; **negative space is generous**
      (peaks at the Air stage).
- [ ] Copy is **de-shaming, calm, sovereign** and carries the five voice lines.
- [ ] Fully responsive 360px→ultrawide; **WCAG-AA** verified; all text passes
      contrast; keyboard + screen-reader usable.
- [ ] **Lighthouse 95+** on all four categories; no CLS; no console errors.
- [ ] Dark mode, `prefers-reduced-motion`, SEO/OG metadata, and the footer
      **crisis-safety note** are all present.
- [ ] With text hidden, the layout/color/motion alone still read as *safe,
      spacious, sacred, grounded.*

---

### How to use this prompt
Paste the whole document into your AI builder, or lead with the **TL;DR Prompt**
and attach §3, §5, and §10 as the non-negotiables. If the builder asks questions,
answer in the brand voice from §7. Iterate stage by stage — get **The Journey**
page perfect first; it sets the standard for everything else.
