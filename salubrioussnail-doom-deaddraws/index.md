# Doom: Reducing Dead Draws Through Synergy

The tightest deck in the Marvel Super Heroes Commander box runs on two engines, so the dead-draw audit is clean. This is my dead-draws video worked on the actual precon, instead of a link to it.

**Faces:** Doctor Doom, King of Latveria {1}{U}{B}{R} · Loki, the Deceiver {1}{U}{B}{R} · Killmonger, Ruthless Usurper (alt). Grixis villains, discard-matters and token copies.

## Two questions, two engines

A card goes dead when you draw it and the deck lacks the pieces that make it work. My fix is an audit: for each card, ask what it accomplishes and what it synergizes with. The Doom deck makes both questions easy, because it wants exactly two things:

- **Engine 1, the discard drain.** Doctor Doom drains the table for two every time you pitch a land, so cards that throw away lands for value feed him.
- **Engine 2, the Loki copies.** Loki makes a tapped attacking copy of another Villain each combat (the token comes in nonlegendary, so even legends are fair game), and Villains that connect draw cards, so any body worth copying feeds him.

Hold each card up to both engines. A card that feeds neither is the dead draw, and that is the slot an upgrade should take. The two commanders draw the category lines for you, which is why this precon is the tightest fit of the four.

## The three swaps

- **Superior Foes of Spider-Man → Green Goblin, Revenant.** A low-payoff card with no home in either engine, for a flying deathtouch Villain that Loki wants to copy and that connects for cards.
- **Molecule Man → Kingpin, Wilson Fisk.** A six-mana 5/5 whose miracle-granting ability wants a different deck, for a payoff that turns your sacrificed Loki tokens into Treasure. An off-plan card becomes part of the copy loop.
- **Damocles Base, Sword of Kang → Sundial of the Infinite.** An off-theme vehicle, for a piece that ends your turn before the delayed sacrifice, so Loki's copies stay as permanent Villains.

## Nothing generically weak comes out

The swaps are the audit in action. Nothing generically weak leaves the deck; what leaves is whatever the discard drain and the copy loop cannot use, and what comes in feeds one engine or the other. That is why Doom is the tightest fit of the four precons, and the deck stops drawing blanks.
