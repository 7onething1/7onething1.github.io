Methodology, sources, and what this does not cover

Source

Corpus collected from 446 Chiron Intuition transcripts through July 7, 2026. Every quoted line is verbatim and links to the exact video. A grounding pass confirmed each read traces to its cited source.

How a reading is assembled

Nothing here is written fresh. Each card's keywords and meaning come from Chiron's curated cheatsheet, her own coherent single-card read rather than a raw transcript grab. Each transition carries the Bunning structural label plus, when it exists, the cheatsheet clarifier note that names the other card. Under a toggle sit the raw verbatim excerpts from individual readings for that pair, ordered by relational specificity (direct, then clarifier, sequence, nearby); these are unedited and can reference a third card or a specific querent, so they are kept as evidence, not as the headline read. The only non-quoted sentences are neutral scaffolding and the attributed Bunning labels.

Coverage

This build indexes 2822 of the 3003 possible unordered pairs (94%) across the 78 cards, from 26,169 combination passages, about 9.3 per pair. A pair she was never recorded reading shows “No recorded passage yet” rather than an invented reading; the structural lens still applies.

The structural lens (Bunning)

Each transition is classified by Joan Bunning's Learning the Tarot, Lesson 15, from card identity alone:
  • Permanent pair — fixed opposites Bunning names, such as the Magician and the High Priestess.
  • Court-card / Ace pair — two courts or two Aces, a contrast of suit and rank.
  • Reinforcing pair — two cards pushing the same energy (here, same suit).
  • Occasional pair — any two cards read together for one instance, no fixed opposition.
